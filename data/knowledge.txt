# Fertilizer Basics
# From Cornell University tutorials

## Basic Fertilizer Materials
Fertilizers are blends of basic materials with N-P2O5-K2O analysis.

Common materials:
- Anhydrous Ammonia: 82-0-0, used for sidedressing N in corn.
- Urea: 46-0-0, topdressed on grasses, can cause high pH near seed.
- Ammonium Nitrate: 33-0-0, for topdressing.
- Ammonium Sulfate: 21-0-0 24-S, mild N source, reduces soil pH.
- Diammonium Phosphate (DAP): 18-46-0, in blends, can cause high pH near seed.
- Monoammonium Phosphate (MAP): 11-52-0, easy on seeds.
- Muriate of Potash: 0-0-60, for topdressing K.

Common blends: 20-10-10, 20-20-0, 10-20-20, 15-15-15, 19-19-19, etc.

## Crop Nutrient Needs
For corn: Typically 110 lbs N/acre, 40 lbs P2O5/acre, 50 lbs K2O/acre.
- Starter: 25 lbs each N, P2O5, K2O in band (e.g., 15-15-15).
- Sidedress: Additional N if needed, based on PSNT.
- Avoid >30 lbs N from urea or >30 lbs P2O5 from DAP in band to prevent injury.

For perennial grasses: 225 lbs N/acre, split applications.
- Topdress with urea.

Guidelines:
- Band starter 2" below and 2" to side of seed.
- No more than 80-100 lbs/acre N+K2O in band to avoid salt injury.
- Use PSNT for sidedress decisions.
